Utility Dive

Q1 2026 — Market Context & Key Themes

Mega-Deal Cycle ContinuesPower & utility deal value hit $142B in 2025 (nearly 2× 2024 per PwC). The AES take-private ($33.4B TEV) is the largest clean energy infrastructure take-private of the current cycle, signaling aggressive private capital deployment into scale generation.
Hyperscaler Vertical IntegrationGoogle's $4.75B Intersect close, Amazon's $83M Oregon site win, and Google's 1.9 GW Xcel partnership mark a structural shift: Big Tech is acquiring generating assets outright, bypassing interconnection queues stretching 7–12 years.
Offshore Wind Political Risk ElevatedTotalEnergies' $1B lease relinquishment (4.2 GW, Mar 23) signals the practical shutdown of federal offshore wind support. Infrastructure funds should reassess offshore exposure and permitting timelines.
OBBBA Reshaping Deal FlowTightened ITC/PTC qualification windows are compressing developer timelines and pushing acquirers toward deployment-ready assets with secured interconnection. Greenfield M&A centers on acquiring queue positions.
Gas Generation Returns as BridgeSB Energy / DOE 9.2 GW gas-fired project (largest in US history) reflects the administration's emphasis on dispatchable generation for AI-driven load growth. Gas increasingly positioned as near-term bridge fuel.
Long-Duration Storage EmergingESS/VoltStorage and Energy Vault/Peak Energy AI storage deals signal growing LDES M&A activity. AI compute workloads require high-cycle storage distinct from grid-balancing use cases, creating new buyer segments.
Supply Chain M&A AcceleratesOBBBA domestic content requirements drive industrial manufacturers into renewable supply chain acquisitions. US-manufactured components now directly affect ITC/PTC credit eligibility.
Scale as Competitive MoatPer PwC / Utility Dive: "scale will determine winners and losers." Renewable asset valuations stabilized after 2024–25 compression — expected to unlock more seller activity in 2H 2026.

Q2 2026 (Through June 30) — Emerging Themes

Gas Dispatchability Commanding PremiumTransAlta's $1B acquisition of Mountain Peak/Canyon Peak (~$3.1M/MW) and Shell's pursuit of PJM gas assets near Data Center Alley signal structural re-rating of dispatchable gas capacity. AI-driven load growth and coal retirements are tightening capacity margins, giving firm peakers new strategic value not seen since the early 2000s.
Enel's US Solar Roll-Up AcceleratesEnel acquired ~1.1 GW of US solar in Q1+Q2 2026 ($1.14B combined), demonstrating the most aggressive US solar buy-side strategy among major European utilities. Q2 deal pricing at $519/kW vs. Q1 at $1,205/kW suggests portfolio differentiation — potential for further consolidation in 2H 2026.
Hyperscaler PPA Activity IntensifiesMeta/Zelestra 180 MW Texas PPA (Jun 9) joins Google's Xcel 1.9 GW Minnesota partnership as Q2 hyperscaler offtake deals. Big Tech now procuring directly across ERCOT, MISO, and WECC simultaneously — creating a persistent bid for renewable capacity across all major US power markets.
Large-Scale Pipeline in PlaySegue's Roosevelt 2 GW solar+storage (NM) is the largest single project sale currently in market. Combined with Acciona's ~400 MW Canada wind process and Ørsted's upcoming US onshore sale (3Q26 formal process), Q3 2026 is shaping up as a significant transaction period for infrastructure fund deployment.
Transmission as Infrastructure Asset ClassTwo MISO transmission projects totaling $1.66B (Iowa/Illinois Sub T + Woodford County) reached preferred proponent stage. As renewable curtailment rises and interconnection queues balloon, transmission is emerging as a standalone attractive asset class with long-term contracted revenue and essential-service characteristics.
Ørsted Exit Watch — US Onshore PortfolioØrsted's pre-launch of its US onshore renewable portfolio sale (formal process 3Q26) would be a major market event. Combined with its offshore wind exits, Ørsted's full US retreat signals the challenges facing European developers in current US policy environment, potentially releasing multi-GW for US and Canadian infrastructure fund acquisition.
European Utility DG Exit WaveThree European utilities have launched or are exploring US DG/distributed solar asset sales in Q2 2026: Engie (Project Endor — full US DG business), CVE North America (~100 MW community solar, NY/MA), and Ørsted US onshore (pre-launch). Convergence with strategic portfolio rationalization by European players suggests a sustained supply of quality operating distributed and onshore renewable portfolios entering market in 2H 2026.
Project Financing at ScaleCypress Creek's $3.5B Steel River financing (1.63 GW+) and Matrix Renewables' $1.3B+ multi-asset structure demonstrate continued deep capital markets appetite for large-scale renewable project financings. Origis Energy's $900M corporate facility signals the maturation of developer-owner-operators accessing corporate credit alongside project-level financing, a structural evolution from the traditional single-project financing model.
Nuclear SMR Re-Emerges as Investable AssetElementl Power's 1.5 GW BWRX-300 SMR project announcement in Ohio (Jun 23) is among the first new US SMR developments, privately financed and targeting 2030 construction start. AI-driven load growth has materially improved the economics case for always-on clean firm capacity. Watch for additional SMR project announcements and potential infrastructure fund interest in nuclear development equity as the asset class matures.
