Market Context & Key Themes — Q1 2026

Mega-Deal Cycle ContinuesPower & utility deal value hit $142B in 2025 (nearly 2× 2024 per PwC), carrying into Q1 2026. The AES take-private ($33.4B TEV) is the largest clean energy infrastructure take-private of the current cycle, signaling aggressive private capital deployment into scale generation.
Hyperscaler Vertical IntegrationGoogle's $4.75B Intersect close (Mar 12), Amazon's $83M Oregon site win, and Google's 1.9 GW Xcel partnership mark a structural shift: Big Tech is acquiring generating assets outright, bypassing interconnection queues stretching 7–12 years in some regions.
Offshore Wind Political Risk ElevatedTotalEnergies' $1B lease relinquishment (4.2 GW combined, Mar 23, 2026) signals the practical shutdown of federal offshore wind support. Infrastructure funds should reassess offshore exposure, permitting timeline assumptions, and capital allocation to offshore development.
OBBBA Reshaping Deal FlowThe One Big Beautiful Bill Act tightened ITC/PTC qualification windows, compressing developer timelines and pushing acquirers toward deployment-ready operating assets with secured interconnection. Greenfield M&A now centers on acquiring queue positions and developer talent.
Gas Generation Returns as BridgeThe SB Energy / DOE 9.2 GW gas-fired project announcement — described as the largest in US history — reflects the current administration's emphasis on dispatchable generation to meet AI-driven load growth. Gas increasingly positioned as bridge fuel in the near-term capacity mix.
Long-Duration Storage EmergingESS/VoltStorage and Energy Vault/Peak Energy AI storage deals signal growing M&A activity in LDES technologies. AI compute workloads require high-cycle, reliable storage distinct from grid-balancing use cases, creating new buyer segments and offtake structures for storage developers.
Supply Chain M&A AcceleratesOBBBA domestic content requirements drive industrial manufacturers into renewable supply chain acquisitions (MacLean-Fogg/OMCO Solar; GameChange/Terrasmart eBOS; First Solar/Oxford PV licensing). US-manufactured components now directly affect ITC/PTC credit eligibility.
Scale as Competitive MoatPer PwC / Utility Dive: "scale will determine winners and losers." Renewable asset valuations have stabilized after 2024–25 compression — analysts expect this to unlock more seller activity in 2H 2026, particularly operating portfolios like Enel's $1B, 830 MW acquisition from Excelsior.
